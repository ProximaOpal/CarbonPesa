Education Hub

Toggle modules to explore climate knowledge

Global Warming

Earth's average temperature has risen 1.1°C since pre-industrial times. Trees absorb CO₂, cooling local microclimates by up to 4°C.

Carbon Sequestration

A single mature tree sequesters ~21 kg of CO₂ per year. Urban forests collectively offset millions of tonnes annually.

Flood Prevention

Tree root systems absorb 10–15 times more water than bare soil, dramatically reducing urban flash flooding risk.

Biodiversity

A single oak tree supports over 500 species. Reforestation is the most cost-effective biodiversity intervention available.

Air Quality

Urban trees remove particulate matter, nitrogen dioxide & ozone — improving respiratory health for millions in cities.

Paris Agreement

Nations pledged to limit warming to 1.5°C. Planting 1 trillion trees could sequester 205 billion tonnes of carbon.

Rainfall Patterns

Forests generate up to 50% of their own rainfall through transpiration. Deforestation disrupts regional precipitation cycles.

Wind Breaks

Tree rows reduce wind speed by 50–70% and protect crops, soils & buildings from erosive wind damage.

Frost Protection

Shelterbelts create microclimates 2–5°C warmer at night, protecting crops from damaging late frosts.

Drought Resilience

Deep-rooted trees access groundwater and release moisture, moderating heat waves & extending growing seasons.

Storm Buffering

Coastal mangroves reduce storm surge height by up to 66%, protecting millions of coastal residents.

Urban Heat Islands

Cities with 30%+ tree canopy cover record temperatures 3–5°C lower than comparable treeless urban areas.

Acacia (Africa)

Fast-growing, drought-resistant nitrogen fixer ideal for dryland restoration. Supports savanna ecosystems across Sub-Saharan Africa.

Moringa

The "miracle tree" — every part is edible or medicinal. Grows in tropical/subtropical zones & purifies water naturally.

Bamboo

Fastest-growing plant on Earth. Sequesters CO₂ at 35× the rate of equivalent timber, while preventing soil erosion.

Neem (Asia/Africa)

Hardy roadside tree with natural pesticide properties. Provides dense shade & thrives in urban pollution environments.

Oak (Temperate)

Keystone species supporting 500+ invertebrate species. Long-lived carbon store with edible nuts for wildlife & humans.

Jacaranda (Urban)

Iconic purple-flowering shade tree perfect for city streets, schools & parks across tropical & subtropical climates.
